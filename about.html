About

I've spent 12 years designing for complexity.

My career has moved through two distinct threads: large enterprise systems at IBM and Microsoft, and the focused intensity of early-stage startups. Both have taught me that the hardest design problems aren't visual: they're about understanding what a person actually needs to do, and then getting out of their way.

Before UX, I founded Signal Cycles, a bespoke bicycle fabrication company featured in the New York Times and recognized at the North American Handmade Bike Show. That work taught me how to design for craftsmanship, how to listen to customers, and how to run a business. Those instincts still show up in how I approach products.

I hold an MA in Industrial Design from SCAD and a BFA in Fine Art from Pacific Northwest College of Art. In 2024 and 2025 I taught Prototyping Products at the University of Texas at Austin School of Design, where students learned to physically prototype in order to better understand the problems they were trying to solve. I'm currently Lead UX Designer at [current company], a browser security startup in Austin, TX.

How I use AI in my workflow

I use Claude Code in the terminal to write, commit, and ship design assets directly to our internal design site via GitHub pull requests. This lets me produce coded prototypes and documentation at engineering speed, without needing a dedicated front-end developer to translate design specs into working artifacts.

The design site is also the foundation for a longer-term investment: a project pipeline where AI agents help with upstream PM work, picking up problems, framing them, and assisting with roadmap scoping. The site was deliberately structured from the start to support this. For a small team, it's a way to multiply capacity without adding headcount.
